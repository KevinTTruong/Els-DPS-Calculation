Chung

[A guardian of Hamel equipped with the power of the Guardian Stone]

Special Ability

Main Article: Freiturnier

Chung's most notable feature is his Cannonball gauge. Chung can reload these Cannonballs in order to gain access to strong commands and to use or strengthen certain Cannonball-consuming Skills, requiring him to keep a steady supply of Cannonballs at all times for optimal play.
He also has the ability to Awaken into his Berserk Mode, which will grant him an unlimited supply of Cannonballs for its duration.
Furthermore, he is uniquely able to enter Heavy Stance, a special form of Super Armor that can allow him to guard against incoming attacks and counterattack while guarding.

Background

Main Article: Hamel of Dawn
  • In-game Version
  • Nexon Version

Name: Prince Seiker
The successor of the Seiker family who are known protectors of the city of Hamel.

When Chung's father, the white Colossus Helputt, becomes corrupted by demon magic and invades Hamel, Chung tries to prevent the invasion but fails.

He joins the El Search Party to become stronger, deciding to go by Chung instead of his real name until he feels worthy.

At birth, the boys of the Seiker family are given a special guardian stone at the family altar.

The weapon created from the Blue Guardian Stone is the 'Destroyer', a huge cannon with muzzle on both sides, and the armor is the 'Freiturnier', which is full body armor. Contrary to his handsome appearance, he freely handles huge cannons, ignoring the opponent's attack and showing a rather tough fighting style that uses his own attack.

He lost his mother quickly and grew up alone under his father, who was always obsessed with country affairs. Although he is a quiet child, he has a very tough fighting style, so he is often mistaken for his usual appearance.

His real name is Prince Seiker. Born into a Seiker family that has protected Hamel, the capital of the Duchy of Senace, from generation to generation, he grew up learning only combat training to protect Hamel.

His father is a hero called 'Hamel's White Colossus'.

When the demons invaded the Senace Principality, his father did everything he could to stop the demons, but he got caught in an evil magic and was controlled by the demons, taking the lead in the invasion of Hamel.

He tried to stop his father and the demons, but it was in vain. He was eventually seriously wounded during the fight,

With the help of Elsword and his party, he managed to escape from the battlefield. While recovering from his injuries, most of the principality of Senace fell to the demons, and he decided that there was no chance of winning against the demons only within Hamel. Leaving it to the Red Knights for a while, he decides to go on a trip with Elsword and the El Search Party to become stronger.

He decided not to use the Seiker name until he became stronger.

He calls himself 'Chung'.


First Class Advancement

Chung is required to be Lv 15 to begin his first class advancement.

Chung can decide between advancing as a Fury Guardian, Shooting Guardian, Shelling Guardian or Cor Guardian.

Enhanced defense and Heavy Stance allows Chung to charge ahead without fear.

With fast magical bullets from Sliver Shooter, the path uses quick long range combo attacks.

Specialize in strategic battle style utilizing artillery and bombs.

Tips and Details

  • Chung falls a bit faster than the other characters, a trait that can often disrupt combos that involve launching.
  • Chung's missiles are classified as physical projectiles, allowing them to bypass reflective effects.
  • Having higher Action Speed will also increase the speed of reloading while standing.
    • Tapping or right after reloading will also help accelerate the process.


Trivia

  • Chung's job path in Lore/Story would be the Comet Crusader path.
  • Chung's official theme song is Hope.
  • Chung is secured by the main cast in the Resiam Outskirts story quest Ill-Kept Secret and officially joins them on their journey during the story quest [Village] El Lady and the Prophecy along with Add and Ara. Prior to joining the main cast, all dungeons will re-use one of 3 generic dungeon clear lines.
  • Many of Chung's skills reuse the crest of Dragon Knight from KOG's defunct game, Grand Chase. The crest can be seen in the sigil of Burst Wolf, Pain of Caladbolg, Rapid Guardian, Exciting Crash, and Tiamat.
    • The crest can also be seen on the floor of the elevator leading to the boss room of Heart of the Ancient Waterway.
    • In Comet Crusader's Hyper Active Tiamat, it shows the sigil appearing when Chung goes into Berserk Mode for the skill. This could be implied that they made the sigil a symbol for Freiturnier.
  • Chung's Korean name "청" literally means "Blue". The nickname was given to him by his father in reference to his eyes which were as blue as the waters of Hamel. Chung typically refers to himself with this nickname, only being called Prince when spoken to formally.
    • "Chung" is canonically a word of Northern Fluone origin, in reference to it being of eastern origin in the real world.
    • It is a misconception that Chung is the prince of Hamel. Prince is not his title; it is his given name.
  • Chung is also known as the White Wolf of Hamel. This reflects the uniformity of his Freiturnier base color.
    • Another reference to his title of "White Wolf" are his pupils, which resemble a dog/wolf's paw mark.
  • Chung's hair is predominantly blonde like his father's, but he has 2 brown spots due to his mother's fully brown hair. The brown spots also resemble animal ears in line with his White Wolf theme.
  • Chung is the first character with a known last name, with it being "Seiker".
  • According to the World Cup 2014 avatar costume for Chung, he seemed to have played water polo in Hamel.
  • According to Chung's Skill Note for Pandemonium - Fear, Chung is afraid of cockroaches.
  • When Chung goes berserk in his reveal trailer of NA, he actually has Iron Paladin's berserk form instead.
  • Chung and all characters that have come after him only have one base skill cut-in as opposed to Elsword~Eve who each have three.
  • Chung was noted for being sick for much of his early life, a trait which he apparently shared with his father.
